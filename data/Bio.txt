Peiyang Song is an incoming Ph.D. student at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU), affiliated with the Language Technologies Institute (LTI) in the School of Computer Science and the Institute for Computer-Aided Reasoning in Mathematics (ICARM). He received his B.S. in Computer Science, with a minor in Robotics, from the California Institute of Technology (Caltech), where he was advised by Prof. Steven Low and Prof. Günter Niemeyer. His research studies machine reasoning in both formal and informal settings. His long-term goal is to build AI systems whose reasoning is as creative as human intuition and as dependable as formal logic. His work has appeared at leading venues including NeurIPS, ICLR, ICML, TMLR, EMNLP, ASPLOS, IEEE Micro, etc. His distinctions include an IEEE Micro Top Pick Award, a Best Paper Honorable Mention at the NeurIPS LAW Workshop, a TMLR Survey Certification, as well as outstanding reviewer awards from EACL and ICLR.
