Peiyang Song is an incoming Ph.D. student at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU), affiliated with the Language Technologies Institute (LTI) in the School of Computer Science and the Institute for Computer-Aided Reasoning in Mathematics (ICARM). He received his B.S. in Computer Science from the California Institute of Technology (Caltech), with a minor in Robotics, where he was advised by Prof. Steven Low and Prof. Günter Niemeyer. His research focuses on building AI systems whose reasoning is both capable and dependable. He works across formal theorem proving, reasoning agents, and the evaluation of reasoning in large language models, often leveraging neuro-symbolic approaches to improve reliability, interpretability, and control in increasingly autonomous systems. His work has appeared at leading venues including NeurIPS, ICLR, ICML, TMLR, EMNLP, ASPLOS, IEEE Micro, etc. His research has received distinctions including a Top Pick Award at IEEE Micro, a Best Paper Honorable Mention at NeurIPS LAW Workshop, an Outstanding Reviewer Award at EACL, and more.
