Peiyang Song is a fourth-year undergraduate studying Computer Science at the California Institute of Technology (Caltech) with a minor in Robotics, advised by Prof. Steven Low and Prof. Günter Niemeyer. He is an incoming PhD student at the Language Technologies Institute in the School of Computer Science, at Carnegie Mellon University. His research focuses on agentic reasoning systems: AI agents that can plan, act, verify, adapt, and improve over time. His work explores how to make reasoning both capable and dependable across formally verified environments and open-ended natural language, often leveraging structured or neuro-symbolic approaches to provide control, interpretability, and reliability in increasingly autonomous systems. His work has been published at leading venues across machine learning, natural language processing, and computer systems—including NeurIPS, ICLR, TMLR, EMNLP, ASPLOS, and IEEE Micro—with oral presentations and awards. His research has received distinctions including a Top Pick of the Year at IEEE Micro and a Best Paper Honorable Mention at the NeurIPS LAW Workshop.
