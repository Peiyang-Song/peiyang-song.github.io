Peiyang Song is a fourth-year undergraduate studying Computer Science at the California Institute of Technology (Caltech) with a minor in Robotics, advised by Prof. Steven Low and Prof. Günter Niemeyer. He is an incoming PhD student at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU), affiliated with the Language Technologies Institute (LTI) in the School of Computer Science, and the newly founded Institute for Computer-Aided Reasoning in Mathematics (ICARM). He is joining MiroMind AI in June, working with Dr. Kaiyu Yang on verifiable reasoning agents. His research explores how to make reasoning both capable and dependable across formally verified environments and open-ended natural language, often leveraging structured or neuro-symbolic approaches to provide control, interpretability, and reliability in increasingly autonomous systems. His work has been published at leading venues across machine learning, natural language processing, and computer systems—including NeurIPS, ICLR, TMLR, EMNLP, ASPLOS, and IEEE Micro—with oral presentations and awards. His research has received distinctions including a Top Pick of the Year at IEEE Micro and a Best Paper Honorable Mention at NeurIPS Workshop.
